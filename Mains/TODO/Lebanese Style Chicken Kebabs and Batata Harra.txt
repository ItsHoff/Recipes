Lebanese Style Chicken Kebabs and Batata Harra

Ingredients
Chicken Kebabs
1lb of chicken breast, cut into 1 inch cubes
5 garlic cloves, minced
1/2 teaspoon paprika
1/2 teaspoon pepper
1/2 teaspoon cumin
1/2 teaspoon salt
1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
1/2 lemon, juiced
3 tablespoons olive oil

Batata Harra
4 firm fresh potatoes, cut into 4th's (3/4inch/2cm dice)
1 teaspoon cumin
1 teaspoon coriander
1/2 teaspoon turmeric
4- 5 tablespoons vegetable oil
salt and pepper

Cooking the chicken kebabs
1. It's best prepare and marinade the chicken 1 day before cooking, but you can let them sit in the fridge for at least.... at least 4 hours. place the chicken in a bowl and add in the garlic, paprika, pepper, thyme, salt, lemon juice, olive oil, mix well and thoroughly and cover bowl with plastic and place in fridge for at least 4 hours (24 hours is best).
2. Preheat broiler in oven (or the grill outside) if the broiler in the oven, take the chicken to the skewers and tip (place) them in a cooking sheet and under the broiler, turning them constantly, for 15 minutes. If the grill outside is used, do the same thing. skewer the chicken, place them on the grill, turning them constantly or from time to time for 15 minutes.

Cooking the Batata Harra
1. Rinse the potatoes until the water is clear. dry them off as well as possible. place potatoes in a bowl and season with the cumin, coriander and turmeric.
2. Pour the oil in a skillet or frying pan so the oil is at a depth of about 1/4 of an inch. add the potatoes and cook them on a medium heat, tossing and stirring them around frequently, until they're golden brown a crispy with a tender soft center.
Serving - Place a few pieces of the chicken on a plate and a side of the batata harra. also serves great with tabbouleh or tzatziki.
